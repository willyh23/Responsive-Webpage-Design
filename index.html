Spatial Analysis of Infectious Disease Outbreaks

Summary

This topic explores how Geographic Information Systems (GIS) are used to track, analyze, and predict the spread of infectious diseases. It covers spatial analysis techniques, real-time mapping, and epidemiological modeling to support public health decision-making.

When it comes to tracking how diseases move through communities, Geographic Information Systems (GIS) let us map out where cases are happening, spot patterns, and even predict where outbreaks might hit next. By combining location data with public health info, GIS helps researchers and decision-makers respond faster and smarter.

Introduction to GIS in Epidemiology

GIS enables the visualization and analysis of disease patterns by linking health data to geographic locations. From mapping COVID-19 cases to tracking malaria outbreaks, GIS helps public health officials understand where and why diseases occur.

Today, modern GIS platforms allow real-time tracking, predictive modeling, and interactive dashboards that support rapid decision-making.
